The Problem

Airport purchasing is not normal purchasing.

Airport teams are often forced to navigate local purchasing rules, federal funding requirements, cooperative contract options, insurance requirements, board approvals, and documentation expectations before they can even decide how to buy. For small teams, that research can take weeks.

Is piggybacking allowed under our local rules?

Do we need to bid, or use an existing contract?

What funding rules apply — AIP, BIL, state, local?

Which documents are required for the file?

Will procurement, finance, or the board question the path?

Are we missing insurance, DBE, Buy America, or grant flags?

The Solution

Turn a purchase question into a procurement path memo.

TarmacSync gives airport teams a structured way to evaluate a high-dollar equipment purchase before the process gets stuck.

STEP 1

Enter the purchase

Equipment category, estimated amount, funding source, urgency, and airport profile.

STEP 2

Review contract options

TarmacSync checks curated cooperative and piggyback contract sources for relevant options.

STEP 3

Flag requirements

Local rules, funding overlays, insurance triggers, approvals, and documentation needs in one place.

STEP 4

Generate the memo

Download a Procurement Path Memo to review with airport leadership, procurement, finance, legal, or grant stakeholders.

Core Output

The Procurement Path Memo.

The memo is designed to help airport teams brief internal stakeholders before committing to a procurement route. It creates a clearer starting point for discussion with procurement, finance, legal, consultants, and funding agencies.
